Dirty Dozen Analysis:
Select and document the human factor conditions that may have contributed to the event, finding, or unsafe condition.

1. Lack of Communication

Missing, incomplete, unclear, or incorrect transfer of information.

2. Complacency

Overconfidence, routine task mindset, or failure to recognize risk.

3. Lack of Knowledge

Insufficient training, unfamiliarity with procedures, or lack of technical understanding.

4. Distraction

Attention diverted by interruptions, multitasking, or environmental factors.

5. Lack of Teamwork

Poor coordination, weak support, or ineffective cooperation between personnel.

6. Fatigue

Reduced performance due to tiredness, workload, shift schedule, or lack of rest.

7. Lack of Resources

Missing tools, equipment, manpower, materials, time, or documentation.

8. Pressure

Time pressure, production pressure, customer pressure, or operational urgency.

9. Lack of Assertiveness

Failure to speak up, stop work, challenge unsafe conditions, or ask for clarification.

10. Stress

Mental, emotional, or operational stress affecting judgment or performance.

11. Lack of Awareness

Reduced situational awareness, not recognizing hazards, changes, or consequences.

12. Norms

Informal practices, shortcuts, or accepted behaviors that differ from procedures.

Summarize the main human factor contributors and their safety impact.
